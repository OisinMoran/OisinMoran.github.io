Bending Spectra








It's CURRENT_YEAR, everything is a spectrum.

Despite some of the furor, this is actually useful. These shades of grey are a step up from black and white thinking, and I'm a big fan of considering things usually seen to be discrete as continuous. [0] Similarly, I also enjoy just generally interpolating between things (another piece on that to come).









But, for a lot of things that are viewed as opposites, the situation isn't that much better when converted to a spectrum. A false dichotomy is just as false when the options are diametrically opposed—in competition for their share of the same axis.

Are you a cat person or a dog person? An introvert or an extrovert? Right-brained or left-brained? Conservative or progressive? Most people aren't fully on either side of any of these, and usually "fall somewhere in the middle". But how can we differentiate between someone who loves cats and dogs equally, and someone who likes neither?

On a simple linear spectrum these would both occupy the exact same point, but represent very different things. So if you find yourself as both, or neither, then whatever you're dealing with is probably not well-represented by a one-dimensional line. Can we do better?

Of course! We simply bend the spectrum, giving each component its own axis, and giving us a much richer space. We now have a separate location for those who love cats and dogs, and those who don't like either. And importantly these two groups are as far apart as can be!
